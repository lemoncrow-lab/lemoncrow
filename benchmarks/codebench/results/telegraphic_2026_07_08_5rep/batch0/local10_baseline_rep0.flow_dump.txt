Two things worth knowing about React error boundaries in general:
- They only catch errors during **render, lifecycle methods, and constructors** of children — not in event handlers, async code, or SSR. Wrap those in `try/catch` yourself.
- If the underlying condition still fails, "Try again" will just re-throw. For transient errors (a failed fetch, a race) it works well; for deterministic ones you'd typically pair retry with resetting the state that caused it.
